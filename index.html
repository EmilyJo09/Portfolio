Who am I?

My name is Emily Johansen. I live in Wisconsin with my boyfriend, Rob, and our dog, Pepper. We have fostered over a dozen dogs in the last three years through Stray Souls Animal Rescue. We enjoy learning new recipes to cook together, handing out with family, and golfing with friends.

Education

I went to the University of Wisconsin - La Crosse to earn my undergraduate degree in Geography with a concentration in GIS and a minor in Spanish. During my time there I worked as a Graphic Designer in the Center for Leadership, Involvement, and Graphics. I also held two internships, one as a GIS Intern for USGS, as well as at the City of Onalaska as an intern for the City Planner. A few years after graduation, I decided to go back to school. Recently I graduated from the University of Wisconsin - Madison's Masters of Web Map Planning and GIS program.

Work

In 2016, I became a Land Use Specialist in the La Crosse County Zoning, Planning, and Land Information office. I spent 5 years in that office, meeting great friends, connections that got me into fostering dogs, and even my boyfriend there. Once covid hit, I decided it was time to move on to a new career. Now I work with the Wisconsin Department of Transportation's Technology Services Section in the Northwest Region. I have been in this position for a year and a half and am excited to see where it takes me!
